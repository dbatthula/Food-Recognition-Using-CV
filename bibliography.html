References

  1. Hokuto Kagaya, Kiyoharu Aizawa, and Makoto Ogawa. "Food Detection and Recognition Using Convolutional Neural Network", ACM International Conference, November 2014.

    Synopsis: This paper presents an approach to food detection and recognition using CNNs, focusing on improving accuracy for complex food images.

    Reliability: High — Published in a peer-reviewed conference.

  2. Ye He, Chang Xu, Nitin Khanna, Carol J. Boushey, Edward J. Delp. "Food Image Analysis: Segmentation, Identification, and Weight Estimation", IEEE, July 2013.

    Synopsis: This paper discusses food image analysis with a focus on segmentation and identification methods, emphasizing the need for precise weight estimation for nutritional assessment.

    Reliability: High — Published in a reputable IEEE conference.

  3. Liu, Pengfei, et al. "DeepFood: Deep Learning-Based Food Image Recognition for Computer-Aided Dietary Assessment." IEEE International Conference on Bioinformatics and Biomedicine, 2016.

    Synopsis: Introduces a deep learning-based food image recognition system using CNNs to assess diet and nutritional intake automatically.

    Reliability: High — Published in an IEEE conference with a focus on healthcare applications.

  4. Christophe Gu, et al. "Food-101 Dataset." Vision Group, ETH Zurich, 2014.

    Synopsis: Describes the Food-101 dataset, which contains over 101,000 images of food across 101 categories. It's widely used for food image classification tasks.

    Reliability: High — The dataset is a major resource for food image recognition research.

  5. "Food2K Dataset." Papers with Code, 2023.

    Synopsis: This dataset consists of 2,000 different food categories, offering a rich source for food classification tasks using deep learning models. It supports advanced computer vision research in food recognition.

    Reliability: Medium to High — Papers with Code is widely recognized in the AI and ML research community for providing benchmark datasets.
